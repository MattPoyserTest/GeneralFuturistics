The situation The gut microbiome is a deeply sophisticated and largely unexplored community of microorganisms. Although it was known to have an effect on gastrointestinal health it hasn’t been until recent years that it has been associated with other traits such as athletic capacity, obesity, and stress response. It has been suggested that novel therapies can be developed targeting the microbiome and its constituents.

The solution
Our research targeted three metabolic pathways through which the microbiome influences mental health and wellbeing. We designed genetic constructs containing the human genes for tryptophan hydroxylase, choline acetyltransferase and glutamate decarboxylase and cloned them into Escherichia coli Nissle.

We also developed a tool for modelling the activity of different gut populations and a microfluidic device to simulate it in vitro. Artist Anna Dumitriu later incorporated our organisms in her project: Hypersynbiont Salon.
